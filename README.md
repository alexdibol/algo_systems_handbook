# Algorithmic Trading Systems Lab  
## Five Agentic Architectures Across Equities, Oil Futures, FX Carry, Credit, and Volatility

Author: Alejandro Reynoso

---

## Overview

This repository contains a compact research and implementation collection for the **Algorithmic Trading Systems Lab**.

The repository includes:

1. Five academic papers  
2. One consolidated handbook  
3. The corresponding Google Colab notebooks  

The five papers cover:

1. Equities  
2. Oil Futures  
3. FX Carry  
4. Credit  
5. Volatility  

Together, these materials present a governance-first approach to algorithmic trading systems. The central thesis is that algorithmic trading is not only about prediction, signals, or backtests. It is a problem of system design. A serious algorithmic trading system must be auditable, traceable, defensible, testable, stress-tested, calibrated, optimized, and subject to human review.

---

## The Handbook

The handbook consolidates the five capstone studies into a single guide.

It explains the common principles that connect the five architectures:

* algorithmic trading as governed system design;
* systems as contracts among agents, gates, artifacts, and human reviewers;
* market-specific trading surfaces;
* hybrid workflows combining deterministic code, statistical inference, generative AI, and human judgment;
* auditability, traceability, stress testing, and committee-readable reporting.

The handbook is intended to provide the conceptual map for the repository.

## The Five Papers

### 1. Equities

The equities paper develops an agentic trading architecture for listed stocks.

It focuses on:

* structured market data;
* unstructured narrative information;
* sentiment and social attention;
* regime-aware signal interpretation;
* portfolio construction;
* transaction-cost realism;
* stress testing;
* audit reporting.

### 2. Oil Futures

The oil futures paper develops a curve-aware trading architecture.

It focuses on:

* futures term structure;
* contango and backwardation;
* roll yield;
* inventory pressure;
* supply shocks;
* maturity-specific agents;
* curve-based allocation;
* stress testing under commodity-market regimes.

### 3. FX Carry

The FX paper develops an agentic system for carry-trade analysis.

It focuses on:

* funding currencies;
* investment currencies;
* interest-rate differentials;
* spot risk;
* volatility-adjusted carry;
* risk-on and risk-off regimes;
* carry-unwind risk;
* portfolio construction across currency exposures.

### 4. Credit

The credit paper develops a governed architecture for credit-spread trading.

It focuses on:

* credit spreads;
* rating-segmented universes;
* spread widening and compression regimes;
* credit migration risk;
* liquidity risk;
* stress testing;
* credit committee-style reporting.

### 5. Volatility

The volatility paper develops an agentic architecture for trading and governing derivatives Greeks.

It focuses on:

* Delta;
* Gamma;
* Vega;
* Theta;
* Rho;
* volatility regimes;
* nonlinear exposure geometry;
* stress testing of derivatives exposures;
* committee-readable risk reporting.

## Colab Notebooks

Each paper is accompanied by a corresponding Google Colab notebook.

The notebooks translate the conceptual architectures into executable educational workflows. They are intended to help readers move from theory to implementation by showing how an auditable agentic trading system can be structured in code.

The notebooks may include:

* synthetic data generation;
* agent definitions;
* shared-state architecture;
* regime classification;
* signal generation;
* portfolio or exposure construction;
* stress testing;
* audit artifacts;
* readable reports.

---

## Core Design Principle

The repository is built around one central principle:

**The model proposes, governance challenges, audit evaluates, and the human remains accountable.**

These systems do not present generative AI as an autonomous trader. Instead, generative AI is used as a bounded interpretive layer inside a broader architecture of deterministic controls, statistical methods, audit logs, stress tests, and human review.
